Service Steps

Service style is to always be - Accommodating . Professional . Proactive . Hospitable . Silent . Confident . Consistent



  • Table should be greeted within 90 seconds. While introducing yourself and offering a beverage, stage the table by moving condiments away from the center to make room for appetizers, extra plates and dessert. Any featured cocktails should be described to the table at this time.



  • The first round (including bar beverages) should be on the table within 3 minutes.



  • You should feature the table while delivering the first-round beverages. Give all the features for the day/night at once, including dessert.



  • Ask for an appetizer order before leaving the table after featuring.



  • If they order an appetizer, plates should be stacked at the edge of the table immediately after ringing the order in.



  • If the guests are ready (or decline an appetizer), this should happen right after featuring as well. If not, it should be done before the appetizer is delivered.



  • 2 bites / 2 minutes after appetizers hit the table. Re-mark any silverware a guest is using so they have fresh silverware for their next course.



  • All appetizer plates and used silverware should be manicured off the table before entrees arrive.



  • 2 bites / 2 minutes after entrees hit the table.



  • Manicuring should be done periodically throughout the meal while trying to bus down to the wood, leaving only one beverage glass per person by the end of the meal.



  • This should happen when the last entree plate is cleared off the table. When asking if they'd like coffee, we do not ask decaf or regular but do ask if they'd like cream.



  • If the table orders dessert, mark them with the proper utensil (ice cream = spoon; everything else = fork). Only one utensil per guest should be brought for desserts. Utensils (like app plates) should be marked immediately after ringing the dessert order in. If the guest orders coffee and dessert, please place the dessert on hold until after the coffee has been delivered.



  • Dropping the check should occur when you quality check the dessert. If the table does not order dessert, it should happen immediately after they decline dessert & coffee (within 2 minutes).



  • Running payments should happen as closely to when they place payment in the boat as possible (within 2 minutes). Make sure to say goodbye and thank you before they leave! Table should be bussed, wiped and re-set in 90 seconds.

Responsibilities

    Homebase Server's Responsibilities



  • A guest request is classified as something a guest needs to begin or continue enjoying their meal (condiments, silverware, etc.). Guest requests are our highest priority responsibility and must be performed within 90 seconds. Please do not pass off guest requests, even if it's for another server's table.



  • Greet each table within 90 seconds of arrival. As you circulate through the restaurant, greet any newly sat table as if it were your own. If you are getting a guest request, a first round or have dirty dishes in your hand, acknowledge the table and let them know you or someone will be with them shortly. Treat our guests as if they have been to our restaurant before. Welcome them back as if they were old friends; there is no need for formality. Begin with a warm smile and a sincere welcome. Offer to bring a beverage, perhaps something from the bar; offer to make a wine suggestion. Inject your personality when interacting with our guests while maintaining your professionalism. The server who took the order should also be the one to deliver the first-round drinks within 3 minutes.



  • With our teamwork system, every server is responsible for running food and bar drinks to any table in the restaurant. The goal is to keep hot food hot and cold food cold. Speed and awareness are crucial. You are the last check on food quality before it reaches the guest. If something does not look correct, ask the expeditor. Be aware of what you are running, how it is cooked, the proper pivot points and accompanying side items. Read the ticket carefully (especially with modifications) before you leave the kitchen or service well. Place food and beverages quietly on the table, directly in front of the guest with the protein facing the guest and the beverages at the top right of their place setting. Be aware if guests need to be remarked with fresh silverware. The last server to drop food should visually scan the table and verbally check if the guests need anything else to enjoy their meal.



  • It is everyone's job to call for, walk and run refills to tables in the restaurant. We never want a guest sitting with an empty glass in front of them.

    Everyone's Responsibilities



  • By taking their own guests' orders the home base server is aware of all, if any, of the guests' requests and needs. This also allows the server to guide the guest and know exactly where and when the guest may need something.



  • ONLY the home base server should inquire how a guests' appetizer, salad and/or entree taste. More than one server doing so unnecessarily interrupts the guest and makes our system look amateurish and unsophisticated.



  • This should be done after all the entree plates are cleared. This ensures more than one person isn't offering and the home base server already knows the guests' preferences, allergies, etc.



  • The guest must be thanked with warmth and sincerity. It is the home base server's obligation to tell the guests what a pleasure it was to wait on them. While dropping the check is the home base server's responsibility, everyone can (and should) run payments for any table.
